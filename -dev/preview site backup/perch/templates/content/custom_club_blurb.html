Instructions:

This is for the description of Holiday Clubs/this year's Holiday Club.

When a club isn't coming up/not planned yet: The club title below can be left blank, and you can delete the Holiday Club logo by deleting the image code from the area below (the part in square brackets). You can simply replace the body text with a generic blurb about our clubs.

When you want to add club information: Simply follow the steps above, in reverse! Add a title, image, and change the blurb to a specific description of the club as needed.

Adding styling/images etc to content:
- Bold/italic can be done by selecting the text you want to bolden/italicise and then clicking the little B or I buttons just above the area where you type. Or, you can add ** to either side of a piece of text for bold, or __ for italics - i.e. "Our club runs from **3-6pm**. Parents __please be aware__."
- Add an image by clicking where you want the image to appear (advisable to only place one between two paragraphs of text) and then clicking the little picture symbol in the same area as the bold and italic buttons above. In the "Select an Asset" box that appears:
- Click an existing item that you want to link to and then click "Use Selected" (should be highlighted green), or
- Click the "Add Asset" button in the top right, and then either drop an image into the indicated box area or click the box area to select the file from your computer.
- Click the image that you just added that should now be displayed in green
- Click "Use Selected" in the top right
- N.B. if you enter this area by accident and want to go back, click the grey (X) icon in the far top right of the "Select an Asset" box, or press ESC on your keyboard.
After this, a piece of code should appear that looks like this: [cms:asset 10 title="Hc 04"]. To get the image to centre and look nice, you'll need to change this to read:

[cms:asset 10 title="Hc 04" class="imagehc"]

Basically just adding class="imagehc" within the square bracket. Make sure there's a space between the title part and the class="imagehc" part so it's composed exactly as above.

If you have any queries, please do not hesitate to contact us.
